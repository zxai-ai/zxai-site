Questions we get asked

The honest Q&A

How much does a front desk employee actually cost per year?
A fully loaded front desk hire runs about $57,800 per year for a medical practice. That includes base salary (around $38,000), benefits at 30 percent, payroll taxes, recruiting costs tied to roughly 2x annual turnover, and onboarding time. The base salary line alone is a fraction of the real cost.
Can AI staff actually replace a front desk employee?
AI staff cover structured front desk workflows: answering calls, booking appointments, confirming visits, rescheduling, sending reminders, and answering common patient questions. A human is still useful for ambiguous situations. Most practices keep one human for judgment calls and let AI staff handle the volume.
How fast can a Front Desk AI staff go live?
About 10 days from kickoff to live. That includes EHR access setup, call routing configuration, script tuning to your practice, and one hour of staff walkthrough. A human hire typically takes 4 to 8 weeks to recruit, onboard, and train to the same baseline.
Does AI staff work 24/7 or just after hours?
24/7/365. That matters because 76 percent of patients who reach voicemail call a competitor instead. After-hours coverage is where AI staff pay for themselves. Every call picked up at 9pm is a patient who would otherwise have chosen another practice.
Is a Front Desk AI staff HIPAA compliant?
Yes. ZxAI runs inside your existing infrastructure and EHR credentials. Patient data never leaves your environment, which means ZxAI is not a Business Associate and no BAA is required. Every SaaS competitor pulls patient data into their own servers and does require a BAA.
What happens if the AI staff gets a question it can't handle?
It escalates. The AI flags the call to your team with full context, the caller's question, and what it already tried. You still get the handoff, but only for edge cases that actually need a human. Routine booking, rescheduling, and reminders are handled without touching your team.
